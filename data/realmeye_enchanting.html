Enchanting

This page is currently a work in progress.

Last updated: Exalt Version 5.11.0.0 (May 2025)

Enchanting is a gameplay mechanic that inbues weapons with different effects and stat boosts, further boosting the power of gear. Players can reroll and upgrade enchantments on their gear through the Enchanter NPC using various kinds of dusts.

The enchanting panel can be accessed through the Enchanter NPC in player’s Vault.

Enchanter

The Enchanter resides in north-east side of the Vault, above the Wardrobe. Players can interact with them to open the Enchanting Interface.

The Enchanter allows players to Reroll and upgrade enchantments.


Key Description
1 Item List - Every item that can be enchanted will appear here with their respective rarity. Only items from your worn inventory or backpack will be displayed. This means that Vault and gift chest items will not appear, nor will items on your other characters. The currently selected item will highlight blue, as shown here.
2 Filters - Allow quick filtering and/or searching between enchantable items (both, tiered, and untiered).
3 Dust Capacity - Shows your current and maximum dust capacity for each type of dust .
Each type has an initial maximum capacity of 2000 which can be further increased with dust capactity upgrades.
4 Slots - Shows what enchantments are currently on the item. You can lock an enchant by selecting the lock icon next to it, which will double the reroll cost but won’t change it when rerolling.
5 Artifacts - Allows you to use any Artifact present in your inventory or backpack when rerolling.
6 Enchantments - The costs of Rerolling enchants are shown here, as well as the cost breakdown, since locking a slot or using artifacts will increase the rerolling cost.

Dusts

Dusts are type of currency that are used in the enchantment process. Currently, there are 3 types of dusts in the game.

Green Dust ( ) Used for rerolling Low tier items or when applying Common Tarot Card Artifacts.
Red Dust ( ) Used for rerolling Medium tier items or when applying Artifacts that boost the chance for an Awakened Artifact. Drops from difficulty 6+ dungeons.
Purple Dust ( ) Used for rerolling Endgame tier items, typically those in Exaltation Level dungeons. Drops from Exalt dungeons & Wine Cellar.

Dust is gained upon completion of a dungeon, and type and amount of dust corresponds to the difficulty of the dungeon completed. Seasonal Characters receive 15% more dust.

Enchantment Rarity

Different Tiers of gear can have different caps on the grade and level of any individual enchantment. Seasonal Characters have a 5% higher chance to roll each slot (so 50% becomes 52.5%)

Shiny items always drop at a grade of rare or above.

Items dropped from advanced dungeons will always drop at a grade of uncommon or above.

Rerolling Enchantments

Enchantments can be rerolled into another enchantment with the respective Dust type ( ) depending on the tier of the item. Higher tiered items will require rarer dust.

Enchantments can reroll into any other possible enchantment the specific gear can have, including the same enchantment of a different level.

You can lock specific enchantments to prevent them from being rerolled, however, each locked slot doubles the cost of rerolling.

Upgrading Rarities

To upgrade an item’s rarity, you can head to the Forge. When crafting an item, the rarity of the created item will be equal to or higher than the minimum rarity of all items used.

For example, using 3 Rare Items and 1 Uncommon Item will ensure that the forged item is at least uncommon rarity. but using 4 Rare items will ensure the forged item is at least Rare Rarity.

Artifacts

You can use Artifacts to influence the probabilities of specific types of enchantment being rolled when rerolling enchantments at the enchanter.

Each artifact affects a generic type of enchantment (e.g. higher chance to roll modifiers that increase WIS), as well as greatly increasing the chance of rolling a specific enchantment, usually a unique or Awakened Enchant.

Enchantments

Enchantments are randomly rolled from the list below, with certain enchantments being locked to specific types of gear. Each enchantment has certain labels that Artifacts work with to either increase/decrease their chances of being rolled.

Most enchantments have restrictions and are incompatible of being rolled together on a single item, For example, an item cannot have ‘Attack Bonus’ and ‘Defense Bonus’ Applied together as they are both ‘Single Stat’ enchantments, but can have ‘Attack Bonus’ and ‘Loot Bonus’ as they are from two different enchantment catagories.

Notes

  • The Percentage HP and MP Regen enchants are superior to their Flat Regen counterparts if the user has a minimum of 800HP or 334MP respectively.
  • The spirits spawned by the Unholy Spirits unique enchant have a range of 6 tiles.
  • For stat boost proc enchants (OnShoot/OnAbility/OnHit), there are some subtle nuances:
    • Stat boost enchants (and their respective cooldowns) are individual to their own item. Likewise, this does mean having multiple boosts of the same stat can potentially be wasted, but if done right, can also be used to increase uptime.
    • If 2 enchants boost the same player stat, activating a stat boost proc after another will not override the previous, even if it has a bigger stat boost. However, the second stat boost will come into effect if there is some duration leftover after the first proc has worn off.
  • The bees spawned by the Living Hive awakened enchant also have a range of 7 tiles, deal 115 damage and attack every 0.4s, resulting in 2875 total damage across their 10s lifetime.

History

  • This mechanic was first described in the April 16th, 2021 Producer’s Letter, said to be released in 2021, but due to balance-related issues, the concept was scrapped and forgotten about.
  • Enchanting came back a year later, reworked and rebalanced, and after a few sessions on the Testing Server, was finally introduced (partially) in Exalt Version 3.4.1.0 (July 2023).
  • Exalt Version 3.4.1.0 (July 2023) introduced the Enchanter, Kogbold Engraving Collection, and MotMG 2023 Engraving Collection.
  • Exalt Version 4.0.0.0 (Aug 2023) introduced tiered equipment enchanting and the Advanced Nest collection. However, the Advanced Nest collection wasn’t available till August 15, 2023, coinciding with the proper release of the Advanced Nest. In that version, Green Dust also started dropping in every Exaltation dungeon.
  • In Exalt Version 4.1.0.0 (Dec 2023), Enchantments were overhauled due to community feedback, going from a open slot based system in which players had to pay dust to upgrade, to a more random system, which removed the upgrade costs, but made item enchantments more random in nature.
  • In Exalt Version 5.11.0.0 (May 2025), Enchantments were once again overhauled. This update removed droppable Engravings and introducing equipment rarities and allowing up to 4 enchantments per item.
  • In Exalt Version 5.12.0.0 (June 2025), 4 additional awakened enchantments were added, Artificial Core Enchantment was reworked and multiple awakened enchantments received new shot sprites.
